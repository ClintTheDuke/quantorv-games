Combat, Weapons & Gear Rebalance in Marathon 1.0.6: Massive Railgun Buffs, SMG Tweaks, and New Weapon Chips

By Clinton Nwezeaku | Updated April 15, 2026

Share this article

If you’re still losing fights… we need to talk 😏

Alright. Be honest (if you may). Have you ever lost a fight in Marathon and immediately blamed:

  • - lag
  • - your mouse
  • - your teammate (probably shout "bot!" at them🙃)
  • - your entire life situation...

    • Yeah… same.
    But after Update 1.0.6? That excuse list is getting shorter. Fast.
    Because Bungie just went in and tweaked weapons, gear, and combat feel like a mechanic fixing every weird noise your car has been making for months.
    Translation: If you lose now… it might actually be you. (I say this with love.)

    Railguns got promoted from “meh” to “main character energy”

    Let’s start with the glow-up nobody expected.
    Railguns.

    Before? Charge → panic → auto-fire → miss → regret.
    Now?

    • No more auto-fire — you control when it shoots (finally)
    • Hold charge indefinitely — peek like a menace
    • Faster charge times — less waiting, more deleting
    • Bigger mags — more chances to ruin someone’s day

    Meanwhile in real life: This is like upgrading from a laggy camera app to one that actually takes the picture when you press the button.
    Simple. Life-changing.

    SMGs, Snipers & Rifles — everyone got “balanced” (read: adjusted attitude)

    This part is basically Bungie walking into the weapon roster and saying: “Alright… some of you are doing too much. Some of you are doing nothing. Let’s fix that.”

    • SMGs — better damage at range, but less “laser beam from across the map” nonsense
    • Bully SMG — got its ego checked (less aim assist, more skill required)
    • Snipers — slower fire rate = stop spamming and actually aim
    • Rifles — smoother reload timing and bug fixes (aka less random frustration)
    • Pistols — fixed stats because apparently numbers matter now

    So yeah… your favorite weapon might feel “different.”
    Not worse. Just… less forgiving.
    Like your WiFi acting up right when you’re about to win a fight.

    Biotoxic Disinjector: from chaos tool to “handle with care” weapon 🔥

    This thing got a full personality reset.
    Before: “Throw everything. Hope it works.”
    Now: “Think before you blow yourself up.”

    • More ammo per shot (nice)
    • Slower grenade speed (less chaos, more intention)
    • Reduced damage radius (no more accidental team wipes… hopefully)
    • Self-damage enabled — yeah… good luck with that

    Developer note literally says “stay safe.”
    Which is game dev language for: “You’re going to hurt yourself with this. We warned you.”

    New Weapon Chips = small buffs, big brain plays

    Now this is where things get interesting.
    New chip families dropped, and they’re low-key game changers:

    • Common Enemy — hit someone → everyone hits them harder (bullying, but strategic)
    • Pocket Change — reload after a kill → free ammo (rewarding clean plays)
    • Exit Buddy — kill someone → speed boost for you and squad (run like you stole something)

    These aren’t flashy… but they stack up fast.
    Meanwhile in real life: It’s like cashback rewards—you don’t notice at first, then suddenly you’re saving money everywhere.

    Yo, check out the update on gameplay systems, new implants, drones, Bungie, did some really cool stuff.

    Optics got nerfed (RIP thermal abusers)

    Let’s pour one out for thermal optics. They’ve been: removed from loot tables.

    Yes. Gone. No more: “Why can this guy see me through everything???”

    • Thermal range reduced where it still exists
    • Sniper optics now have trade-offs (less aim assist, slower ADS)
    • Variable zoom fixed (no more lying stats)

    Translation: If you see someone now… it’s because they actually saw you. Crazy concept.

    Gear tweaks that quietly change fights

    Some of these changes look small… until they ruin your entire strategy.

    • Claymores — smarter detection, less buggy damage (more consistent “gotcha” moments)
    • Self-revive kits — rarer now (so stop playing like you have infinite lives)
    • Depleted self-revives (solo only) — helpful, but you can’t hoard them

    Meanwhile in real life: This is like your favorite shortcut route suddenly having traffic lights. You can still use it… just not as recklessly.

    Combat feel improvements (aka things that should’ve been fixed ages ago)

    Now for the subtle stuff that actually matters:

    • Better enemy footstep audio — no more surprise attacks from ghosts
    • Less fall damage while downed — dying twice is now slightly harder
    • Fixed reticle bugs — your aim won’t randomly betray you mid-fight

    These aren’t flashy changes… but they make every fight feel cleaner.
    Like switching from a cracked phone screen to a brand new one.

    Final thoughts: Adapt… or get farmed

    This update didn’t just tweak numbers.
    It changed how fights play out.

  • - Railguns are smarter
  • - SMGs require more control
  • - Snipers demand patience
  • - Gear punishes careless play

    • Basically: The game is slowly shifting from “chaos wins” to “smart play wins.”

    So… be honest: Did this update make you better… or expose you? 😏
