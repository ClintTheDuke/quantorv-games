If you thought making money in games was just “kill enemy → collect loot → repeat,” then Crimson Desert looked at that idea and said: “cute… but no.” The economy in this game is surprisingly deep, chaotic, and sometimes feels like it was designed by someone who really enjoys spreadsheets at 2 AM.

The Reddit community has been experimenting heavily with different money-making methods, and the results are… let’s just say players are either becoming rich gods or accidentally broke philosophers depending on their strategy.

Understanding the Core Money System

Read About the Infinite Money Glitch in Crimson Desert.

At the heart of the system is the banking and investment mechanic. Players can unlock a bank strongbox for a small fee of 100 silver. From there, things escalate quickly into gold bars, risk strategies, and what can only be described as “virtual Wall Street chaos.”

The conversion rate is simple: 1 gold bar = 500 silver. That means even getting started requires smart early-game farming or… questionable moral decisions (more on that later).

This helpful guide gives exposure on The Best Ways to Play Crimson Desert

Most players begin by scraping together around 600 silver just to get their financial “career” started. And yes, apparently stealing is considered a “career starter” in this economy.

The Banking Strategy That Everyone Argues About

The most controversial method in the community is the banking investment loop. According to the guide, players can use a high-risk investment strategy to generate massive returns every few in-game days.

The strategy goes like this:

  • Deposit gold bars into the bank strongbox
  • Set investments to high risk
  • Save the game just before payout cycles
  • Reload if the outcome is bad
  • Repeat until profit appears like magic

It’s basically capitalism… but with a “load save” button.

Players claim this system can generate up to +40 gold bars per cycle, though some believe there’s a hidden cap or soft limit. Others suspect the bank is secretly judging your financial decisions.

Risk Levels and Wealth Management

The community has basically created a “financial rulebook” for the game:

  • High Risk: under 72 gold bars (fast gains, chaotic losses)
  • Medium Risk: under 200 gold bars (balanced growth)
  • Low Risk: up to 300 gold bars (slow but stable wealth)

High risk is basically gambling with extra steps. Medium risk is for players who like sleeping peacefully. Low risk is for people who have accepted they are now accountants.

Eventually, players accumulate so many gold bars that even their storage systems start to panic. At that point, you are no longer playing an RPG — you are running a medieval hedge fund.

Alternative Money Methods (The Fun Chaos Options)

Not everyone agrees the bank is the best option. In fact, the Reddit community has proposed several alternative methods that are arguably more fun… and slightly more chaotic.

One of the most popular alternatives is farming “Golden Apple Hedgehogs.” These strange creatures spawn repeatedly in certain locations and drop valuable golden apples.

Players discovered that you can farm them quickly by leaving the area and returning, causing them to respawn. It’s less “economy simulation” and more “hedgehog recycling program.”

Other money-making methods include:

  • Gambling in poker-style mini-games
  • Pickpocketing NPCs while wearing a mask
  • Mining gold ore and crafting bars manually
  • Exploiting investment cycles with save/load strategies

Honestly, the game sounds like it can’t decide whether it wants to be an RPG or a criminal simulator.

Why Everyone Needs So Much Money

You might be wondering: “Why are players so obsessed with money?” The answer is simple — Crimson Desert is expensive.

Late-game systems require huge investments in:

  • Camp upgrades
  • Research systems
  • Donations to churches
  • NPC trust-building through gifting

Yes, you can literally throw gold at people until they like you. Real life marketing teams are taking notes.

Because of this, money isn’t just for buying gear — it’s a core progression system that affects almost everything.

The Hidden Reality of Money Farming

While the banking method looks powerful, many players criticize it for being slow, repetitive, and heavily dependent on save reloading.

Loading screens alone have reportedly caused emotional damage to some players (not scientifically proven, but emotionally confirmed).

On the other hand, farming methods like hedgehogs or gambling are faster but riskier. It really depends on whether you prefer:

  • Slow wealth simulation with spreadsheets
  • Fast chaotic grinding with questionable life choices

Both are valid. Both are slightly unhinged.

Final Thoughts

The economy in Crimson Desert is surprisingly deep and flexible. Players can choose between structured investment systems, chaotic farming loops, gambling mechanics, or straight-up theft.

What makes it interesting is that no single method is universally “best.” Instead, players are constantly experimenting, optimizing, and occasionally breaking the game’s economy in creative ways.

Whether you become a careful investor, a hedgehog farmer, or a full-time gambler, one thing is clear: in Crimson Desert, money isn’t just power — it’s survival, progression, and sometimes comedy.

So choose your path wisely… or don’t. The bank probably won’t judge you (much).
