Marathon Update 1.0.6 Overhaul: New Progression Systems, Contracts, and Player Rewards Explained

By Nwezeaku Clinton | Updated April 15, 2026

Table of Contents

This isn’t a patch… it’s a lifestyle change 😏

You know those updates where you log in, skim the patch notes, and go, “cool… I guess my gun reloads 0.2 seconds faster”?
Yeah… this is NOT that update.
Marathon 1.0.6 basically walked into the room, flipped the table, and said: “Hey… what if we changed how you progress, how you earn rewards, AND how you make friends mid-match?”
And honestly? It’s kind of chaotic… but in a “wait, this might actually be genius” way.
This isn’t just new content. This is Bungie quietly rewiring how you play the game.

Meet C.A.R.R.I.—aka the system that rewards you for not being a menace

Let’s start with the headline feature: C.A.R.R.I. (CyberAcme Runner Reinforcement Initiative). Yes, the name sounds like a corporate AI that emails you at 3AM. But what it actually does? Pretty interesting.
C.A.R.R.I. rewards you for:

  • Completing contracts — shocking, I know… doing objectives now matters..
  • Helping teammates — teamwork? In a PvPvE game? Who allowed this?
  • Exfiling successfully — aka “leave alive and get paid”
  • Playing solo — finally, introverts get love too

And the currency you earn? CyberAcme Commendations. Which is basically Bungie saying: “Congrats for not being toxic. Here’s money.”

CyberAcme Commendations: The new grind… but actually worth it

Now here’s where things get spicy.
Instead of grinding endlessly for random loot like your life depends on it, you now earn commendations that actually do something useful. You can spend them in the new: C.A.R.R.I. Armory.

  • Salvage Crates — random loot drops (aka gambling, but legal)
  • Reputation Packs — boost your faction progress without suffering
  • Daily refresh stock — so you check back like it’s your favorite food spot

Meanwhile in real life: This is like your boss finally paying you extra for actually doing your job… instead of just saying “good work” and disappearing.

“Stay Together” — Bungie accidentally invented friendship

Okay, this might be the most underrated feature in the entire update. Stay Together lets you team up with random players you just survived with. Yes. You can now:

  • Survive a chaotic match
  • Build trust mid-exfil
  • Form a new Crew instantly

And the best part?
Even if you die… as long as ONE teammate escapes, you can still stay connected.

Which basically means: “You died… but your friendship lives on.”
Honestly? That’s kind of wholesome for a game where people usually shoot first and regret nothing.

Rewards Pass got buffed (yes, even the free one)

Now let’s talk rewards.
Because nothing hurts more than grinding a pass and getting… disappointment.
This update adds:

  • New Runner Shell styles — because drip matters
  • WSTR Shotgun style — finally, your weapon can look as dangerous as it feels
  • Profile emblem — flex quietly but effectively

Breakdown:

  • Premium Track — Thief, Assassin, Destroyer (aka the cool kids club)
  • Free Track — Recon + extras (respectable… not embarrassing)

Translation: Even if you’re not paying, Bungie isn’t leaving you in the dust this time.
Small win. Big vibes.

Bungie also added an update on Combat and Weapon Rebalance which might actually affect your current play style. check it out to update ❤️

Beginner map finally makes sense (no more getting bullied at Level 2)

Let’s be real for a second. Nothing kills motivation faster than: - Logging in as a new player - Getting absolutely destroyed in 30 seconds - Questioning your life choices Well… Bungie noticed. The Perimeter (Beginner) map is now:

  • Solo-only — no squads farming you for sport
  • Lower risk encounters — fewer “surprise deaths”
  • Available until Level 12 — actual learning time
  • 5 new beginner contracts — structured progression

Meanwhile in real life: This is like finally getting a tutorial that doesn’t assume you already know everything.

Shocking concept.

Contracts & progression just got… less painful

Contracts used to feel like: “Do 17 things, hope it counts, cry if it bugs out.”

Now?

  • Better progress sharing across crews — teamwork actually works
  • More faction reputation rewards — progress feels faster
  • Solo players get boosted gains — finally not punished for playing alone

Also, you can now: claim completed contracts even if you’re playing as Rook.
Which is one of those small changes that quietly fixes a lot of frustration.

Armory economy: less grind, more control

The Armory isn’t just a shop anymore—it’s basically your new progression hub.
Instead of praying for RNG like it’s a religion, you now:

  • Earn commendations through gameplay
  • Spend them on what you actually need
  • Check rotating stock like a daily ritual

It’s still grindy… but now it feels like you’re making decisions instead of just rolling dice.

Quality-of-life fixes that quietly save your sanity

Now for the unsung heroes of the update:

  • Voice chat toggle — no more awkward “can you hear me?” moments
  • Exfil timer fix — no more fake “I escaped!” heartbreak
  • UI access improvements — Rewards Pass easier to find
  • Bug fixes across contracts & codex — less confusion, more clarity

That exfil fix alone?
That’s emotional damage prevention.

Because nothing hurts like thinking you made it out… and the game says “nah.”

Final thoughts: This update changes how you play (for real)

Marathon 1.0.6 isn’t trying to impress you with flashy additions. It’s doing something more dangerous: making the game feel better to play long-term.

  • - Progression is smoother
  • - Rewards feel meaningful
  • - Teamplay is encouraged
  • - Solo players aren’t punished

    • It’s like Bungie finally asked: “What if we made the grind… actually enjoyable?”

    Quick warning: If you ignore the new systems, you’ll fall behind fast. This update rewards smart play—not just aggressive play.

    And honestly… that’s a good thing.

    The update also featured Gameplay systems, which include: implants, drones and major fixes these changes may interest you.

    So tell me: Are you playing solo and stacking commendations… or are you finally trusting random teammates? 😏
