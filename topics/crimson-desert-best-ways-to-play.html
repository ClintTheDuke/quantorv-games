The Ultimate Ways to Play Crimson Desert : A Comprehensive Guide

By Nwezeaku Clinton | Loading...

Table of Contents

Crimson Desert is not your typical open-world RPG. While many players expected another fantasy adventure inspired by games like Assassin’s Creed, Dragon’s Dogma, or The Witcher, Pearl Abyss created something far more experimental. The game mixes survival systems, deep exploration mechanics, intense combat encounters, crafting, climbing, and large-scale battles into one gigantic experience.

Unlike many modern RPGs, Crimson Desert does very little hand-holding. Tutorials are limited, important mechanics are hidden behind experimentation, and progression depends heavily on curiosity. Players who rush through the game often miss crucial mechanics, powerful equipment, and hidden systems that dramatically improve the experience.

This guide covers the ultimate ways to play Crimson Desert efficiently while also maximizing enjoyment, exploration, and long-term progression.

Explore Hernand Thoroughly Before Leaving

One of the biggest mistakes beginners make is rushing out into the wilderness immediately after the introduction. Hernand serves as the foundation of the early game and acts as your main support hub for many hours.

The town contains vendors, beginner quests, crafting systems, inventory upgrades, and useful tutorials disguised as side activities. Spending time in Hernand teaches players how gathering, survival, crafting, and progression systems work together.

Several NPCs offer “favor” quests that reward inventory expansion slots. These upgrades are incredibly important because Crimson Desert uses a restrictive inventory system where tools permanently occupy space.

Players should also speak to every merchant they encounter. Many vendors sell Small Bags for cheap prices, permanently increasing storage capacity. This becomes essential later when carrying multiple tools, healing items, crafting materials, and weapons.

Understand Inventory Management Early

Inventory management is one of the hidden survival mechanics in Crimson Desert. Every tool occupies inventory space, including pickaxes, shovels, axes, mallets, brooms, and secondary weapons.

Unlike traditional RPGs where equipped items disappear from inventory weight calculations, Crimson Desert keeps them active in your storage. This means careless inventory management can quickly become frustrating.

However, players should never throw away important tools simply to save space. Fast travel is limited during the early hours, and replacing a missing tool may require traveling long distances across dangerous territory.

Smart players keep a balanced inventory containing:

  • Gathering tools
  • Healing supplies
  • Crafting resources
  • Backup weapons
  • Palmer Pills
  • Food and stamina items

Learn the Fast Travel System as Soon as Possible

Fast travel in Crimson Desert works very differently from most open-world games. Instead of clearly marked teleport locations in every town, players must discover hidden structures scattered throughout the world.

When exploring, pay close attention to suspicious map locations covered by clouds or marked with strange energy descriptions. Many of these locations hide ancient travel points.

Once discovered, players must activate them using the lantern mechanic. These fast travel points dramatically reduce travel time and make gathering, questing, and exploration much easier.

Thankfully, later updates from Pearl Abyss added more fast travel points near major settlements and camps, improving overall accessibility.

Master Combat Positioning

Combat in Crimson Desert can spiral out of control extremely quickly. Small skirmishes often turn into large-scale battles as nearby enemies join the fight dynamically.

The best strategy is controlling positioning rather than simply attacking aggressively.

Archers should always be eliminated first because ranged attacks constantly interrupt movement and healing opportunities. Players should circle around the battlefield, isolate ranged enemies, and reduce pressure before engaging melee opponents directly.

Dodging, spacing, and stamina management are far more important than button mashing. Many boss encounters punish reckless aggression heavily.

One of the smartest features in Crimson Desert is the ability to retreat from difficult encounters. If a boss becomes overwhelming, players can leave, improve their gear, unlock abilities, and return later without replaying entire missions.

Build Trust With Your Horse

Horses are more than transportation in Crimson Desert. They are long-term companions tied directly to mobility progression.

By petting your horse frequently, trust increases over time, unlocking advanced riding mechanics such as improved speed and horse drifting.

Many players miss this feature entirely because the interaction prompt only appears while holding the L1 button near the horse.

Since the world is enormous and fast travel is limited initially, improving horse mobility significantly improves exploration efficiency.

Never Waste Palmer Pills

Palmer Pills are among the most valuable consumables in the game. These items allow players to revive instantly during combat with partial health restored.

The game does a poor job explaining their importance, causing many players to accidentally waste or sell them.

Smart players reserve Palmer Pills for:

  • Boss battles
  • Large raids
  • High-risk exploration zones
  • Late-stage combat encounters

Since boss encounters can last a long time, using a Palmer Pill at the right moment may prevent losing significant progress.

Be Extremely Careful With Keys and Locked Doors

One of the strangest mechanics in Crimson Desert involves locked doors. Earlier versions of the game automatically consumed keys whenever players interacted with locked entrances.

This created frustrating situations where rare keys were wasted opening useless rooms.

Fortunately, updates added confirmation prompts before using keys, but players should still remain cautious.

Some locked locations contain:

  • Rare equipment
  • Crafting materials
  • Hidden lore
  • Treasure chests
  • Secret shortcuts

Others contain almost nothing valuable at all.

Choosing when to spend keys becomes an important resource-management decision.

How to Find the Shotgun Shield

One of the most useful hidden weapons in Crimson Desert is the Shotgun Shield.

Players can locate this powerful equipment piece inside the Helmara enemy camp situated just outside Tashkalp.

To reach the location efficiently, travel to the camp positioned between the letters “H” and “K” on the world map, directly beneath the Crimson Desert region label.

Sneaking is highly recommended because the camp contains numerous hostile enemies capable of overwhelming underprepared players.

Once inside the camp, head toward the lower section of the area. Near a staircase surrounded by posters and structures, players will discover the Shotgun Shield waiting nearby.

This weapon is particularly effective for players who enjoy aggressive close-range combat while maintaining defensive versatility.

How to Obtain the Crimson Warden’s Bow

Another excellent hidden weapon is the Crimson Warden’s Bow.

To obtain it, travel to the Demeniss region and search the area located below the letter “N” on the map.

Near the waypoint at the end of the path, players will find a barn tower positioned north of the city walls.

Climb to the upper section of the tower to access the entrance door. After entering the structure, turn right to locate a treasure chest containing the Crimson Warden’s Bow.

This bow is especially valuable for players focusing on ranged combat builds or stealth-oriented gameplay styles.

Invest in Stamina and Mobility Skills

Exploration in Crimson Desert depends heavily on stamina management. Climbing functions similarly to modern Zelda games, where players consume stamina while scaling walls, cliffs, and mountains.

Investing points into stamina early dramatically improves exploration freedom. Players should also prioritize unlocking Double Jump in the Spirit skill tree because it opens access to hidden ledges and traversal routes.

Much of the world is vertically designed, rewarding players who invest in movement capabilities.

Reaching elevated locations often leads to:

  • Hidden treasure
  • Rare crafting materials
  • Secret weapons
  • Fast travel points
  • Exploration rewards

Avoid Overusing Abyss Artifacts

Gear enhancement becomes increasingly expensive as the game progresses. Eventually, upgrading equipment requires Abyss Artifacts, which are also essential for permanent character progression.

These artifacts improve:

  • Health
  • Stamina
  • Combat skills
  • Character growth systems

Because of this, players should avoid wasting valuable artifacts upgrading low-tier gear too early.

Instead, focus on replacing outdated equipment naturally while saving rare resources for endgame progression.

Eventually, stronger gear becomes worth investing in, but early-game restraint pays off significantly.

Always Craft Exactly What NPCs Request

Crafting quests in Crimson Desert are surprisingly strict.

If an NPC requests a specific item, players must provide the exact version requested. Enhanced or upgraded alternatives will not count toward completion.

Many players accidentally waste valuable materials creating superior versions of recipes only to discover the NPC refuses them entirely.

Precision matters in Crimson Desert’s crafting system, so always follow quest instructions carefully.

Understand the Save System

The save system in Crimson Desert initially confuses many players because the top section of the menu contains rotating autosaves.

Manual saves can only be created in separate empty save slots further down the menu.

Players who fail to notice this sometimes believe the game lacks manual saving entirely.

Creating multiple manual saves is highly recommended, especially before major boss encounters or dangerous exploration sessions.

Final Thoughts

Crimson Desert succeeds because it refuses to follow predictable RPG formulas. The game constantly rewards curiosity, experimentation, patience, and exploration.

While some mechanics feel intentionally obscure, that mystery becomes part of the experience. Hidden fast travel systems, secret weapons, difficult bosses, and exploration-based progression all contribute to a world that feels handcrafted rather than overly guided.

Players who take their time, study the systems carefully, and fully embrace exploration will discover one of the most rewarding fantasy RPG experiences in recent years.
