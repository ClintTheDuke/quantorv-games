Best Abyss Gears in Crimson Desert and How to Get Them

By Nwezeaku Clinton | Updated 2026

Table of Contents

If Your Build Feels Weak… Yeah, It’s Probably Your Gear 😏

If your character in Crimson Desert is struggling, it’s not “bad luck.” It’s your gear. Specifically… the lack of Abyss Gear.

These aren’t your average stat-boost trinkets. Abyss Gears are the kind of upgrades that make enemies question their career choices mid-fight. The kind where one attack turns into five, explosions happen for no reason, and suddenly you’re the problem.

The only downside? The game makes you work for them. Of course it does.

The Best Abyss Gears (And Why You Need Them)

Here’s your cheat sheet to becoming dangerously overpowered:

  • 🟢 Greysoul Howling (Chapter 9)
    Adds a delayed shadow strike when using Turning Slash.

    Translation: you hit once… then the game hits them again just to be petty.

    It’s like sending a “just remembered this argument” text hours later — unnecessary, but effective.
  • 🟢 Shadow Claw (Legendary Wolf Questline — House Celeste, Hernand)
    Adds a heavy slashing attack that scales with your weapon refinement.

    The stronger your weapon, the more this thing goes from “nice bonus” to “absolute menace.”

    Meanwhile in real life: You upgrade something and it still somehow runs worse.
  • 🟢 Groundsurge (End of Chapter 7)
    Fires a line of elemental damage that explodes and launches enemies.

    Yes, launches. As in… enemies stop being your problem and start being gravity’s problem.

    Honestly, this one feels illegal. In a good way.
  • 🟢 Crow’s Pursuit (Chapter 5 Boss Reward)
    Every heavy attack triggers a crow storm.

    A storm. Of crows. Because apparently normal combat wasn’t chaotic enough.

    You swing once and suddenly it’s a nature documentary — narrated by violence.
  • 🟢 Moon Slashes (Lunar Judgement Questline — House Elemore, Demeniss)
    Stack Crescent, Half, and Full Moon Slash to turn light attacks into triple energy beams.

    LIGHT attacks.

    At this point, your “basic combo” becomes more destructive than most boss mechanics. Completely fair, obviously.
  • 🟢 Hound’s Claws (Hernandian Parish of Solumen Questline)
    Turns a spin attack into a massive whirlwind AoE.

    You’re no longer fighting enemies — you’re blending them.

    This is what happens when a shieldbreaker move decides it wants a promotion.
  • 🟢 Infinite Arrows (+2 & +3 — Pailune Militia Questline)
    Gives you a 100% chance to not consume arrows.

    Infinite ammo. No farming. No stress.

    This is the gaming equivalent of finding out snacks are free. Life-changing.

How to Actually Get Them (Without Losing Your Sanity)

Quick reality check — you’re not just picking these up casually. Most of these are locked behind:

  • Story chapters (so yes, you have to actually progress 😒)
  • Questlines with names that sound harmless… but aren’t
  • Boss fights that suddenly remember how to be difficult

And since many of these come with spoilers, the game basically says: “Go explore, and good luck figuring it out.”

Which is fun… until you’re 20 minutes deep into a quest wondering if you took a wrong turn three objectives ago.

Pro Tips (Because You Will Mess Something Up)

  • Don’t ignore questlines.
    If it sounds important, it probably unlocks something ridiculous.
  • Upgrade your weapons early.
    Some gear scales with refinement — meaning weak weapons = wasted potential.
  • Mix and match smartly.
    Stacking effects is where things get broken… in the best way.
  • Be patient.
    Rushing through chapters will leave you underpowered and confused. Classic combo.

Why Abyss Gear Changes Everything

Once you start stacking these, the game shifts.

Enemies that used to stress you out suddenly feel like background extras. Combos become chaos. Fights become shorter. And you start wondering why you ever struggled in the first place.

It’s not even the stats — it’s the effects. The extra hits, explosions, storms… it all adds up into one thing:

You become unfair.

Final Thoughts

If you’re serious about Crimson Desert, Abyss Gear isn’t optional — it’s the difference between surviving and absolutely dominating.

Just be ready to earn it. The game will test your patience, your timing, and occasionally your sanity.

Quick warning: once you get used to these upgrades, going back to normal gear feels like trying to run a modern game on a 2008 laptop. Painful. Very painful.

Alright — be honest… which one are you grinding for first? Or are you about to collect all of them and become the problem? 🔥
