How to Get the Greysoul Howling Abyss Gear in Crimson Desert (Without Losing Your Sanity)

By Nwezeaku Clinton | Updated 2026

Table of Contents

So You Want a Ghost Buddy That Fights for You?

Let me guess—you heard about the Greysoul Howling Abyss Gear and thought, “Yeah, I definitely need a ghost warrior backing me up in fights.” Same. Because honestly, why struggle alone when you can summon a phantom to do half the work while you pretend you planned everything?

This gear is one of those “wow, this changes everything” items. It literally summons Goyen’s phantom to attack enemies. Basically, you get a built-in teammate who doesn’t complain, doesn’t need healing, and won’t steal your loot. 10/10 companion.

Step 1: Unlocking the Quest (a.k.a. The Game Testing Your Patience)

Before you even think about touching this gear, the game makes you work for it. You’ll need to unlock the “Thinning Blade” quest in Chapter 9. And no, it’s not just handed to you like free candy.

You’ll have to cleanse a shrine (because apparently you’re also part-time janitor now) and inspect plaques at four statues. Once you do that, boom—four quests unlock, including the one you actually care about.

At this point, I was already tired. But hey, no pain, no phantom.

Step 2: The Journey to Spire of the Sun

Next, you head to the Spire of the Sun. Sounds majestic, right? Yeah… until enemies start jumping you like you owe them money.

You fight your way through until you reach the Nest of Valor in the Abyss. And that’s where things get serious. Because waiting for you there is Goyen—the guy whose ghost you’re about to recruit (after beating him up first, of course).

Classic RPG logic: defeat someone, then use their power. Totally normal.

Step 3: Beating Goyen Without Crying

Now let’s talk about Goyen. This guy LOVES his shield. Like, if shields were a personality trait, that would be his entire identity.

The trick here is simple: stay aggressive. Keep hitting him until his stagger bar fills up. Once he’s stunned, that’s your moment. Go all in like you’ve been saving your frustration for this exact moment (because you have).

I won’t lie—this fight took me a few tries. Okay, more than a few. But eventually, Goyen goes down, and you get that sweet victory feeling… mixed with relief that you don’t have to fight him again.

Step 4: The Secret Loot (Don’t Miss This!)

Here’s where a lot of players mess up—they beat the boss and just leave. Don’t be that person.

After defeating Goyen, look to the right side of the arena near the water. There’s a staircase leading up to a hidden room. Inside, you’ll find the Fated Shadow sword sitting there like it’s been waiting just for you.

And guess what? The Greysoul Howling is embedded inside it.

At this point, I felt like I had just found treasure… because I basically did.

Step 5: Wait… Why Can’t I Use It Yet?

Here’s the twist: you don’t automatically get to use the Greysoul Howling on your favorite weapon. Nope. The game hits you with one more step.

You need to visit a witch.

Yes, an actual witch. Not for a curse (hopefully), but to extract the Abyss Gear from the Fated Shadow and transfer it to your weapon of choice.

Think of it like gear surgery. Slightly magical. Slightly confusing. But very necessary.

Finally… Was It Worth It?

Short answer: absolutely.

Once you equip the Greysoul Howling, combat feels completely different. You’ve got Goyen’s phantom jumping into fights, dealing extra damage, and making you look way cooler than you actually are.

It’s one of those upgrades where you go, “Yeah… I’m never taking this off.”

Plus, let’s be honest—having a ghost warrior on your team is just cool. No strategy explanation needed.

Final Thoughts (And a Small Warning)

Getting this gear isn’t exactly easy. You’ll fight, explore, get lost, maybe rage a little—but that’s part of the fun.

Just remember:

Don’t skip the hidden room. Seriously. I’m still thinking about how painful it would be to miss that sword and realize it later.

Anyway, go grab that Greysoul Howling and start summoning chaos. And if Goyen’s ghost starts carrying you in fights… just pretend that was always the plan.
