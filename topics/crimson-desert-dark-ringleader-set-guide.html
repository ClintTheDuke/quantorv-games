Crimson Desert Tips and Tricks: How to Get Dark Ringleader's Complete Setpiece

By Nwezeaku Clinton | Updated 2026

Table of Contents

So You Want to Look Like a Boss?

Half the reason we grind gear in games isn’t stats… it’s drip. If your character doesn’t look like they could casually overthrow a kingdom while sipping tea, what are we even doing here?

Enter the Dark Ringleader set in Crimson Desert — a collection so clean it practically screams, “Yes, I am the main character.” The catch? The pieces are scattered across Hernand like the devs decided to play hide-and-seek after three cups of coffee.

But don’t worry. I’ve done the annoying part so you don’t have to rage-quit after your third waterfall.

Step-by-Step: Collecting the Full Set

Here’s where things get fun… and slightly disrespectful to your patience.



  • 🟢Gloves — Cloud Mist Cave
    Ah yes, the classic “walk behind the waterfall” trick. Because apparently every fantasy world shares the same interior design logic.

    You’ll need to use the stab ability to pass through the waterfall. No stab, no entry. It’s basically the game saying, “Come back when you’re cool enough.”

    Meanwhile in real life: You can’t even push a stuck door open without embarrassing yourself.


  • 🟢Helmet — Shadowheart Grotto
    Located behind — wait for it — another waterfall. At this point, waterfalls are less “scenic” and more “suspicious.”

    Head down to the bottom area and look carefully. If you miss it, don’t worry… we’ve all stared directly at loot before and still walked past it like NPCs.


  • 🟢 Chest Piece — Blade Cave
    Hidden behind a northern waterfall. Yes. Again. I wish I was joking.

    This piece actually matters though — it gives extra damage reduction, which basically means enemies hit you and you just… mildly disagree.

    This is the armor equivalent of saying, “That all you got?”


  • 🟢 Cloak — Echoing Tunnel (Near Fort Perwin)
    By now you already know the drill. Go right. Find waterfall. Question your life choices.

    But this one’s worth it — high movement speed and ice resistance. Translation: you move like a legend and don’t freeze like your WiFi during a storm.

Pro Tips (Before You Lose Your Mind)

Let me save you from unnecessary suffering:

  • Waterfalls are not decoration.
    If you see one, assume there’s loot behind it. Always. No exceptions. The devs clearly have trust issues.
  • Bring the right abilities.
    That stab ability? Yeah… not optional. Showing up without it is like bringing a spoon to a sword fight.
  • Check corners like your life depends on it.
    Because sometimes… it actually does. Or at least your sanity does.
  • Don’t rush.
    Sprinting through these areas will have you missing gear and then googling angrily 10 minutes later.

Why This Set Is Actually Worth It

Okay, aside from looking absolutely ridiculous in the best way possible, the Dark Ringleader set is low-key stacked:

  • Damage reduction from the chest piece = fewer “why am I dead?” moments
  • Movement speed from the cloak = you finally stop moving like you’re stuck in mud
  • Ice resistance = winter areas stop bullying you

Basically, it turns you from “random adventurer” into “this guy again?” — and trust me, enemies will feel it.

The Real Challenge (Spoiler: It’s Not the Enemies)

Here’s the funny part — none of this is actually hard. The real boss fight is your attention span.

Because Crimson Desert doesn’t just hide items… it hides them like it owes you money.

You’ll walk past clues, second-guess yourself, go in circles, and eventually realize the answer was literally behind flowing water the entire time. It’s humbling. Painfully humbling.

Final Thoughts

So yeah — if you want the Dark Ringleader set, just remember:

  • Trust no waterfall
  • Check everything twice
  • And never assume the game is being reasonable

One wrong turn and you’re backtracking like you forgot your wallet at home… except the “wallet” is a legendary armor piece and your enemies are very committed to ruining your day.

Quick warning: don’t try to speedrun this unless you enjoy frustration as a hobby. Take your time, explore properly, and actually look behind things (wild concept, I know).
