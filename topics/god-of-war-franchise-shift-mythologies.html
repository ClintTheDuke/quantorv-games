From Norse to Mayan? God of War’s Next Move Could Change the Franchise Forever

By Clinton Nwezeaku | Update April 17, 2026

Share this article
Link copied to clipboard!

Table of Contents

Kratos Is Not Done Traveling… Apparently

Just when you thought Kratos might finally sit down, relax, and maybe, just maybe, go five minutes without fighting a god…

Nope.

Now rumors are saying the next God of War might dive into multiple mythologies.

Chinese. Japanese. Mayan. Wow.

At this point, Kratos isn’t just a god-slayer… he’s basically on a world tour 😏

This could either be the boldest move the franchise has ever made… or the moment things get a little too ambitious.

From Greek Chaos to Norse Drama… What’s Next?

Let’s not forget—this series has already pulled off one of the cleanest transitions in gaming history.

We went from:

  • Greek mythology Pure chaos. Rage. Everything was on fire—sometimes literally.
  • Norse mythology More grounded. Emotional. Still violent… just with feelings now.

And it worked. Like, really worked.

So now the idea of jumping into multiple mythologies at once?

That’s a full sprint into "unknown" territory.

Multiple Mythologies = Massive Possibilities

Okay, let’s talk about why this is actually exciting.

If done right, this could open up some wild opportunities:

  • New gods, new enemies 🔥 Different cultures = completely different types of threats. No more predictable boss fights.
  • Fresh environments From icy Norse lands to jungles, temples, and ancient cities—variety goes crazy.
  • New weapons and abilities Because you just KNOW Kratos isn’t sticking to the same old gear forever.

It’s like upgrading from one map… to an entire globe.

And suddenly, the stakes feel way bigger.

But Let’s Be Honest… This Is Risky

Alright, here’s the part nobody wants to say out loud.

More doesn’t always mean better.

Sometimes it means:

  • Too many ideas fighting for attention
  • Stories that feel rushed
  • Worlds that look great but lack depth

You know that feeling when a game throws 50 features at you… and you end up using like 5?

Yeah. That.

If God of War tries to juggle too many mythologies without giving each one proper focus, things could get messy fast.

Meanwhile in Real Life…

This whole situation feels like:

  • Trying to watch three different shows at once and pretending you understand all of them
  • Ordering way too much food because everything looks good… then realizing you can’t finish anything
  • Opening 20 tabs on your browser and forgetting why you opened half of them

Ambition is great… until it starts overwhelming you.

World-Building Could Go Absolutely Crazy

Now here’s the flip side—if they actually pull this off?

We’re looking at one of the most insane world-building setups ever.

Imagine:

  • Different mythologies interacting with each other
  • Conflicting beliefs and gods clashing
  • Entire regions with unique rules and histories

This isn’t just expanding the map—it’s expanding the entire concept of the game.

And Kratos being stuck in the middle of all that chaos?

Yeah… that sounds about right.

Fan Expectations Are Already Sky-High

Let’s not pretend the pressure isn’t real.

God of War fans are not casual about this series.

They expect:

  • Strong storytelling
  • Meaningful character development
  • Combat that feels satisfying every single time

So when you introduce something this big?

The expectations go from “this should be good” to:

“This better be PERFECT.”

No pressure, right?

The Real Question: Can It Balance Everything?

This whole idea comes down to one thing:

Balance.

Because to make this work, the game needs to:

  • Give each mythology enough depth
  • Keep the story focused
  • Not overwhelm the player

Basically, it needs to feel like one cohesive experience—not three different games stitched together.

And that’s not easy.

Like… at all.

But If It Works? Game Over

If they actually nail this…

We’re talking about a franchise evolution on another level.

If this is not just a mere rumour then we might really be in for a treat.

The kind where future games start getting compared to it like:

“Yeah, but does it handle multiple mythologies like God of War did?”

That’s when you know something changed.

And honestly? This series has already proven it can take risks and come out stronger.

So betting against it might not be the smartest move.

Final Thoughts: Bold Move or Too Much Too Soon?

This rumor is exciting. No doubt about it.

But it’s also one of those ideas that sits right on the edge between:

  • “This is genius”
  • and “This might be too much”

And which side it lands on?

That’s going to depend entirely on execution.

Quick warning: The more ambitious a game gets, the harder it is to deliver everything perfectly. Expectations are already through the roof—and hype can be brutal if things don’t match it.

But remember.... regardless of the hype, or the "expert reviews" all that matters is your personal experience playing the game

But if there’s one franchise that’s earned a little trust… it’s this one.

Alright, your turn— Do you think God of War should stick to one mythology at a time… or go all-in on this multi-mythology madness?
