What is Crow’s Pursuit in Crimson Desert?

In Crimson Desert, some Abyss Gear abilities simply add damage. Others add flashy effects. Then there is Crow’s Pursuit — an ability that basically tells enemies, “Personal space is no longer your right.”

Crow’s Pursuit is one of the more powerful Abyss Gear skills players can unlock in the game. It is attached to the Tauria Curved Sword and functions as a ranged-style attack for melee weapons. In other words, players can slash enemies from a distance while looking unnecessarily cool doing it.

Naturally, this has made the skill highly sought-after among Crimson Desert players, especially those who enjoy turning combat encounters into cinematic boss fights starring themselves.

▶

The Main Way to Get Crow’s Pursuit

The primary method for obtaining Crow’s Pursuit is by progressing through Chapter 5 of Crimson Desert.

Players will eventually encounter the infamous Crowcaller boss during the “Black and White” sub-chapter, usually around the Hernand region.

Now, the Crowcaller is not exactly the type of enemy that politely waits for the player to prepare snacks before battle. The fight can become intense quickly, especially for players who entered the encounter feeling overconfident after defeating random wolves five minutes earlier.

Defeating the Crowcaller rewards players with the Tauria Curved Sword, which contains the Crow’s Pursuit ability.

Once the sword is obtained, players can extract the Abyss Gear ability from it and transfer it to other compatible weapons.

Basically, the sword becomes less important than the terrifying power hidden inside it.

How to Extract Crow’s Pursuit

After obtaining the Tauria Curved Sword, players will need to extract the Crow’s Pursuit Abyss Gear before embedding it into another weapon.

This process is usually handled through the Abyss Gear system using Witch NPCs located across different regions.

These Witches are essentially magical upgrade technicians. Instead of fixing phones, they remove ancient destructive powers from legendary weapons and casually hand them back to players.

Players can:

  • Extract Crow’s Pursuit from the Tauria Curved Sword
  • Embed the skill into new weapons
  • Add sockets to compatible gear
  • Manage multiple Abyss abilities

Once equipped properly, Crow’s Pursuit gives melee combat a dangerous ranged edge, making it useful for aggressive builds and fast-paced fights.

Alternative Ways to Get Crow’s Pursuit

Interestingly, some players discovered that Crow’s Pursuit is not limited to the Crowcaller boss reward.

Crimson Desert appears to include several alternative acquisition methods, although some of them rely heavily on luck, persistence, or suspicious goblin economics.

1. Camp Exploration Missions

Players can reportedly obtain Crow’s Pursuit blueprints through camp exploration missions.

One mission often mentioned is the “Fallen Abyss Exploration” located in the Ross region.

Sending camp units on exploration missions can occasionally reward rare Abyss Gear materials or blueprints.

Of course, sometimes the camp returns with absolutely nothing useful except emotional disappointment and extra inventory clutter.

2. Areciel, the Witch of Strength

Another possible method involves purchasing materials or blueprints from Areciel, the Witch of Strength.

Depending on player progression, Areciel may offer crafting-related resources tied to rare Abyss Gear.

This means players who constantly ignore NPC dialogue might suddenly realize that the mysterious witch they skipped earlier was actually selling extremely valuable gear upgrades the entire time.

3. Back Alley Goblin Merchants

Now comes the weird part.

Multiple players reported finding rare Abyss Gear through shady goblin-style merchants operating in back alley shops near Hernand.

These vendors apparently sell random rare abilities including:

  • Crow’s Pursuit
  • Frost Spike
  • Hail
  • Abyssal Rage

Nobody fully understands why these goblins suddenly gain access to legendary powers capable of flattening armies. They just do.

Some players believe the inventories unlock after specific story progression milestones, while others think the system is partially random.

Either way, checking black market vendors regularly may eventually reward patient players.

Honestly, Crimson Desert turning goblin alley merchants into endgame gear dealers somehow makes perfect sense.

Why Crow’s Pursuit is So Popular

Crow’s Pursuit has become popular because it changes how melee combat feels.

Normally, melee builds require players to stay dangerously close to enemies. Crow’s Pursuit helps bridge that gap by adding ranged offensive pressure without sacrificing aggressive playstyles.

The ability also looks visually impressive during combat. Players are naturally drawn toward anything that makes enemies explode while dramatic effects fill the screen.

It pairs especially well with fast weapons and Abyss combo builds, allowing players to stack multiple destructive effects together.

Final Thoughts

Crow’s Pursuit is easily one of the coolest Abyss Gear abilities currently available in Crimson Desert.

While the main method involves defeating the Crowcaller boss during Chapter 5, players also have alternative opportunities through camp missions, Witch crafting systems, and suspiciously generous goblin merchants.

The ability’s ranged combat style, flashy effects, and strong offensive utility make it worth the effort for most players.

So if enemies have been surviving a little too comfortably lately, Crow’s Pursuit may be exactly what the player needs to remind them that distance is merely a suggestion.
