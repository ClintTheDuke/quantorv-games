8 Types of Video Games Every Gamer Should Know

By Nwezeaku Clinton | Updated March 13, 2026

Table of Contents

Understanding Video Game Genres

If you’ve been gaming for more than ten minutes, you’ve probably heard people casually toss around words like “RPG,” “FPS,” or “battle royale.” To a seasoned gamer, these are just everyday terms. But to someone new to gaming, they might sound like secret codes whispered by caffeinated nerds in a dark basement.

In reality, these labels are simply video game genres. They help categorize games based on how they’re played, the mechanics involved, and the kind of experience they deliver. Think of genres like food categories: pizza, burgers, sushi, and tacos all fall under “food,” but each has its own flavor and personality.

The gaming industry has exploded over the last two decades, creating hundreds of sub-genres and hybrid experiences. But if we strip everything down to the basics, there are eight core types of video games that every gamer should understand.

Whether you're a casual mobile player, a console warrior, or someone who still calls the controller a “Nintendo,” this guide will walk you through the major video game categories — with examples and a little humor along the way.

1. Action Games

Action games are the adrenaline junkies of the gaming world. These games focus heavily on reflexes, quick decision-making, and fast gameplay that keeps your heart rate somewhere between “excited” and “did I drink too much coffee?”

Players often dodge attacks, perform combos, and react quickly to enemies appearing out of nowhere. The pacing is usually intense, and the gameplay rewards skill, timing, and sometimes a little bit of reckless bravery.

Common action game mechanics include:

  • fast combat systems
  • platform jumping and movement challenges
  • enemy encounters that demand quick reflexes
  • boss battles that test your patience and sanity

Popular examples include action-heavy franchises where dodging, shooting, or slicing your way through enemies is the main objective. If a game makes you shout “HOW DID THAT HIT ME?” at your screen, whether it's a mobile pvp or console, chances are you're playing an action game.

2. Adventure Games

Adventure games are all about storytelling, exploration, and puzzles. Instead of focusing purely on reflexes, these games reward curiosity and problem-solving.

Think of adventure games as interactive stories where you guide the main character through mysteries, strange worlds, and occasionally questionable life choices.

Many adventure titles encourage players to:

  • explore environments
  • solve puzzles
  • interact with characters
  • uncover story elements piece by piece

Adventure games often feel like playing through a movie or novel. Sometimes the biggest challenge isn’t defeating enemies — it’s figuring out which lever opens the ancient temple door without accidentally summoning a giant rolling boulder.

3. Role-Playing Games (RPGs)

Role-playing games, commonly known as RPGs, allow players to step into the shoes of a character and experience a world from their perspective. These games often feature character progression systems, skill upgrades, and deep storytelling.

In RPGs, players might:

  • level up characters
  • upgrade abilities and equipment
  • complete quests
  • interact with complex storylines

One of the biggest joys of RPGs is watching your character grow stronger over time. Early in the game you might struggle to defeat a single enemy. Ten hours later you’re basically a walking natural disaster with legendary armor and questionable moral decisions.

RPGs can be turn-based, real-time, or open-world, but the core idea remains the same: immersive storytelling combined with character progression.

4. Strategy Games

Strategy games reward brains over reflexes. Instead of reacting quickly, players must plan carefully, manage resources, and outsmart opponents through tactical decisions.

These games often involve building armies, managing civilizations, or controlling battlefields where every move matters.

Typical strategy mechanics include:

  • resource management
  • unit positioning
  • long-term planning
  • tactical decision-making

If action games are a boxing match, strategy games are chess — except the chess pieces occasionally launch rockets at each other.

Victory usually depends on planning ahead and adapting to your opponent's moves. In other words, strategy games are where patient gamers shine and impulsive button-mashers quietly suffer.

5. Simulation Games

Simulation games attempt to replicate real-world activities as closely as possible. These games allow players to experience situations they might never encounter in real life — like running a city, piloting an aircraft, or managing an entire farm without ever touching soil.

Simulation games often focus on realism and detailed systems that mirror real-world mechanics.

Examples of simulation experiences include:

  • city-building simulators
  • life simulation games
  • vehicle and flight simulators
  • business or management simulators

Some simulation games are incredibly relaxing. Others quickly turn into chaotic spreadsheets where you're wondering why your digital citizens are angry about taxes again.

6. Sports Games

Sports games bring real-world athletics into the digital arena. From football and basketball to racing and extreme sports, these games simulate competitive physical activities.

The appeal is simple: you can experience the thrill of competition without needing an actual stadium or the athletic ability to sprint for ninety minutes without collapsing.

Sports games usually focus on:

  • realistic player mechanics
  • team management
  • competitive multiplayer modes
  • career progression systems

For many players, sports games are the ultimate couch-competition experience. Few things test friendships faster than losing a match because your opponent scored a goal in the final five seconds.

7. Puzzle Games

Puzzle games challenge players to think logically and solve problems. These games range from simple brain teasers to complex puzzles that require serious concentration.

Puzzle mechanics often include:

  • pattern recognition
  • logic challenges
  • environmental puzzles
  • spatial reasoning

A fantastic example of good puzzle games is the classic 2048 game by Gabriel Cirulli. You can also try out the Modern 2048: Grid Evolution by Quantorv Games

Puzzle games may look simple on the surface, but they can be incredibly addictive. Many players start thinking they’ll play for five minutes — and suddenly it’s two hours later and they’re still trying to solve the same level.

These games prove that sometimes the biggest challenge in gaming isn’t defeating enemies — it’s defeating your own brain.

8. Multiplayer Online Games

Multiplayer online games connect players from around the world into shared digital spaces. Instead of competing against computer-controlled enemies, players face real opponents or cooperate with teammates.

This category includes many competitive and cooperative experiences such as:

  • battle royale games
  • team-based shooters
  • massively multiplayer online worlds
  • competitive arena games

The unpredictable nature of human opponents makes multiplayer games exciting — and sometimes slightly chaotic. One match you’re playing with highly coordinated teammates. The next match someone on your team is trying to cook dinner while playing with one hand.

Despite the occasional chaos, multiplayer games remain one of the most popular forms of gaming today.

Why Understanding Game Genres Matters

Knowing the different types of video games helps players discover new experiences that match their interests. Someone who enjoys strategy games might also enjoy simulation titles, while action lovers might find excitement in fast-paced multiplayer shooters.

Genres also help developers communicate what kind of experience a game offers. When players hear “RPG” or “strategy game,” they immediately understand the core mechanics they can expect.

As the gaming industry continues to evolve, many modern games blend multiple genres together. For example, an action game might include RPG elements, or a strategy game might introduce multiplayer mechanics.

These hybrid experiences create exciting new gameplay styles that push the boundaries of traditional genres.

Final Thoughts

The gaming world is incredibly diverse, but most titles can be traced back to a few core categories. Understanding the eight types of video games helps players navigate the massive landscape of modern gaming.

Whether you prefer fast-paced action, strategic planning, immersive storytelling, or relaxing simulations, there’s a genre designed to deliver exactly that experience.

And as gaming technology continues to evolve, these genres will keep expanding, mixing, and reinventing themselves in ways we probably can’t predict yet.

So the next time someone asks, “What kind of games do you play?” you’ll have a much clearer answer — and maybe even a few recommendations ready.
