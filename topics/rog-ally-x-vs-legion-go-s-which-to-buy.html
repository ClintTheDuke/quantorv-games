ROG Ally X vs. Lenovo Legion Go S: Which Handheld Should You Buy?

By Clinton Nwezeaku | 2026-05-26

Table of Contents

The Handheld Battle You're Probably Overthinking

So you're ready to buy a powerful Windows handheld but can't decide between the ROG Ally X and the Lenovo Legion Go S? You're not alone. In 2026, these two beasts are going head-to-head, and the choice comes down to one big question: Do you want raw power with some setup headaches, or a more console-like experience right out of the box?

Whether you're a busy parent stealing gaming sessions on the couch or a traveler who hates lugging a laptop, this guide will help you decide. Let's dive in with zero boring tech jargon — and maybe a few laughs along the way.

Design & Comfort: Which One Feels Better in Your Hands?

Imagine holding these devices for two hours straight. The ROG Ally X feels compact and premium, like a refined sports car. It's lighter and easier to carry around all day. But the Legion Go S? It's a bigger boy with an 8-inch screen that makes games feel more immersive — think of it as the comfortable couch versus a sporty bucket seat.

The Legion Go S wins on ergonomics for long sessions thanks to better back buttons and that massive display. However, it can feel a bit bulky in your bag. If you have smaller hands, you might find yourself joking that the Legion Go S is training your grip strength for free.

Display Showdown: Size vs Quality

The Legion Go S brings a larger 8-inch 120Hz panel that pulls you deeper into your games. Colors are vibrant and the extra real estate makes strategy titles and open-world adventures pop. The ROG Ally X counters with a sharp 7-inch 1080p 120Hz screen that's no slouch, but it feels more portable.

If you're the type who wants cinema vibes on the go, the Legion Go S is calling your name. Just don't expect it to fit easily in a small jacket pocket.

Performance: Raw Power vs Real-World Play

The ROG Ally X often edges out in benchmarks thanks to better-optimized drivers and higher RAM speeds. It handles demanding AAA titles with more consistent frame rates when you crank up the TDP. The Legion Go S (especially SteamOS Edition) holds its own nicely, particularly in the Z2 Go configurations.

But here's the thing: both can run modern games beautifully at 720p-1080p. You're buying a handheld, not a desktop replacement. The real winner depends on whether you prioritize peak performance or balanced, enjoyable playtime.

Software Wars: Windows Pain vs SteamOS Bliss

This is where things get spicy. The ROG Ally X runs full Windows 11 with Armoury Crate as its control center. It's powerful but can feel clunky. You'll deal with driver updates, background processes, and occasional "why won't this work?" moments that make you want to throw it across the room (don't actually do that).

Now, the Legion Go S SteamOS Edition changes the game. It ships with a streamlined, console-like experience powered by a customized SteamOS build (or Bazzite in some setups). Resume your game in seconds. No random Windows updates ruining your vibe. It's the closest thing to a Steam Deck alternative with more power.

Armoury Crate gives you deep customization on the Ally X, but Bazzite/SteamOS on the Legion Go S just feels smoother for pure gaming. If you hate tinkering, the Legion Go S SteamOS Edition will make you smile every time you power it on.

Game Launcher Compatibility & Anti-Cheat Reality

Both devices support major launchers (Steam, Epic, Game Pass), but Windows on the ROG Ally X wins for universal compatibility. Want to play every competitive shooter? The Ally X has your back.

The Legion Go S SteamOS Edition excels with your Steam library and works great with Heroic Games Launcher for Epic/GOG titles. However, anti-cheat limitations hit harder here. Games like *Call of Duty* or *Valorant* often refuse to run properly on SteamOS builds without workarounds. If multiplayer competitiveness is your main thing, the Ally X is safer.

For single-player, indies, and co-op? You'll barely notice the difference on the Legion Go S.

Battery Life & Portability

The ROG Ally X generally offers better battery efficiency thanks to its hardware tuning. You can squeeze out longer sessions in lighter games. The Legion Go S is no slouch, especially on SteamOS, but its bigger screen can drain the battery faster at high settings.

Both are portable, but the Ally X wins if you travel light. The Legion Go S feels more like a premium portable console.

Price & Value in 2026

The Legion Go S SteamOS Edition often starts at a friendlier price point (around $500-700 depending on config), making it a strong Steam Deck alternative. The ROG Ally X sits higher, especially fully loaded, but justifies it with premium build and performance.

You get what you pay for — but you also pay for what you value most.

Final Verdict: Which One Should You Buy?

Here's the no-BS answer: If you want maximum compatibility, competitive multiplayer, and don't mind occasional Windows quirks, grab the **ROG Ally X**. It's the powerhouse that rarely lets you down.

If you crave that pick-up-and-play magic, beautiful big screen, and hate setup drama, go for the **Lenovo Legion Go S SteamOS Edition**. It's the more relaxing, console-like experience that feels like a true next-gen handheld.

Think about how you actually game. Are you deep into competitive shooters or do you mostly enjoy story-driven titles and indies? Your answer decides the winner.
