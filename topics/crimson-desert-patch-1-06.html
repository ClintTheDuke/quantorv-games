Crimson Desert Patch 1.06.00 Has Players Panicking — Here’s What Actually Happened

By Clinton Nwezeaku | Updated May 11, 2026

Crimson Desert Patch Notes 1.06.00 Finally Arrive

The latest Crimson Desert patch notes 1.06.00 have officially arrived! this update feels like Pearl Abyss looked at the community wishlist and said, “Fine… take everything.” Players are getting massive gameplay changes, new systems, quality-of-life improvements, combat updates, bug fixes, and perhaps the most dangerous feature of all: the ability to tame giant angry animals and ride them into battle like a fantasy Uber service.

Patch 1.06.00 is one of the biggest updates the game has received so far. From the brand-new Crimson Desert mount system to the highly requested extraction mechanics for refinement materials, there is a lot for players to unpack. Some fans are celebrating. Others are still emotionally recovering after thinking their mounts vanished into another dimension after updating the game.

Special Mounts Completely Change the Mount System

The biggest addition in this update is easily the new Crimson Desert mount system. Players can now tame and register special animals as mounts after earning their trust. And no, petting a tiger once does not magically make it your best friend. Apparently, even fantasy animals have trust issues.

Players can now tame animals including:

  • Wolves
  • Bears
  • Lions
  • Tigers
  • Boars
  • Camels
  • Raptors
  • Deer
  • Iguanas
  • Kuku Birds

Some creatures can simply be fed, while more aggressive beasts require players to subdue them before taming becomes possible. So yes, players now have to fight a wild bear first before convincing it to become a loyal companion. Truly the healthiest friendship-building process imaginable.

The update also introduces a dedicated Special Mounts inventory tab, allowing players to equip saddles and mount gear directly from the interface. Saddleries across different cities now sell mount equipment tailored for specific creatures.

However, the update also caused temporary panic across the community. Shortly after the patch launched, several players believed their mounts had disappeared entirely because they no longer appeared in the mount wheel.

Thankfully, the mounts were not deleted. They were simply moved to the new Special Mounts tab. One player jokingly admitted they “had a panic attack” before discovering their wolf mount was still alive and well. Somewhere out there, thousands of digital bears narrowly escaped abandonment.

The New Crimson Desert Extraction Feature Explained

Another major highlight from the update is the new Crimson Desert extraction feature. This new system allows players to recover materials used in equipment refinement without destroying the equipment itself.

Previously, upgrading gear felt like entering a dangerous relationship where you invest everything and hope it works out. Now players finally have a safety net.

Using the extraction system at smithies, players can roll back refinement levels and recover some of the materials used during upgrades. This is especially useful for players experimenting with builds or trying to recover rare materials from failed investments.

The patch notes explain that:

  • Rare materials return at 100%
  • Common materials return at around 70%
  • The equipment itself is not destroyed
  • Players can extract one level or return gear fully to base refinement

This new refinement rollback system is expected to heavily impact the in-game economy and make high-level experimentation less punishing.

The update specifically mentions recovery for items tied to the Crimson Desert refinement materials system, including resources such as ores, bloodstones, Artifacts, and Aeserion’s Scale.

Abyss Gear and Abyss Artifact Improvements

The update also introduces major changes involving Crimson Desert Abyss Gear and Crimson Desert Abyss Artifact systems.

Certain equipment pieces, including Visiones gear, can now be enhanced and fitted with sockets for Abyss Gear customization. This gives players more flexibility when optimizing builds for combat or PvE encounters.

In addition, players can now obtain Abyss Gear and special rewards through the newly added claw machine at the Laughing Marionette. Yes, Pearl Abyss added an actual claw machine into the game. Because apparently dungeon crawling was not stressful enough already.

The claw machine rewards include:

  • Abyss Gear
  • Abyss Artifacts
  • Furniture items
  • Lighting decorations
  • Special cosmetic headgear

Meanwhile, players farming strongholds also received good news, as the patch increases the chances of obtaining Abyss Gear Blueprints in certain locations.

Crimson Desert Oongka Skills Receive Major Combat Upgrades

Fans of Oongka are eating well this patch. The new Crimson Desert Oongka skills update introduces several new unarmed combat abilities under the “Fists” skill category.

Pearl Abyss also improved chain attacks connected to Roundhouse Kick while enhancing general movement and combat animations for unarmed fighting styles.

Oongka players also received fixes for several frustrating issues, including:

  • Explosive Leap canceling before landing
  • Airborne combat interruptions
  • Problems with Elemental Meteor Kick

The combat improvements make Oongka feel much smoother overall, especially during aggressive combo-heavy encounters.

Meanwhile, the Crimson Desert Damiane update confirms that unarmed combat skills for Damiane are planned for the next patch. So if players were hoping to punch monsters dramatically while looking stylish, their moment is coming soon.

Quality-of-Life Features Players Have Wanted for Months

Patch 1.06.00 also delivers several smaller improvements that players have been requesting for quite some time.

One of the most noticeable additions is the new Display Sheath toggle. Players can now choose whether sword sheaths appear visually on their character. It may sound small, but fashion is the true endgame in RPGs. Everyone knows this.

Another standout addition is the new Night Tone Mode, which softens overall colors and brightens darker areas for nighttime gameplay sessions.

Additional quality-of-life updates include:

  • Skill menu search function
  • Improved fishing UI
  • Inventory bulk actions
  • Better climbing controls
  • Improved ranged targeting
  • Stackable furniture items

Players can also purchase the new Sigil of Valor item, allowing pet dogs to attack enemies. Finally, the loyal dog companion can stop watching the chaos and actually join the fight.

Community Frustration Over NPC Faction Quests Continues

Despite all the exciting additions, one issue continues to frustrate players across Steam discussions, YouTube comments, Reddit threads, and Facebook groups.

Several players report being stuck on NPC faction quests tied to “Tales after Demeniss.” According to the complaints, required NPCs simply refuse to appear even after players spend hours roaming the expected areas.

One frustrated player explained they had spent over two weeks repeatedly searching for the missing NPCs without success. At this point, some players probably know every rock and tree in the region better than the developers themselves.

However, another player suggested the NPC appearances may depend on the in-game time cycle. According to their experience, some NPCs only appear during specific times of day.

If this is true, the issue may not entirely be bug-related but rather poorly communicated quest mechanics. Either way, players are hoping Pearl Abyss addresses the confusion soon.

Performance Fixes and Final Thoughts

Outside gameplay additions, the patch also includes a huge number of technical fixes and performance improvements. Players received HDR improvements, bug fixes for boss fights, animation corrections, stability updates, rendering fixes, and better optimization across platforms.

Mac players especially benefit from improved HDR detection and reduced GPU crashes, while console players received fixes tied to adaptive triggers and fullscreen stability.

Overall, Crimson Desert Patch Notes 1.06.00 represents one of the game’s biggest updates so far. The new mount mechanics, extraction systems, combat improvements, and Abyss Gear changes all significantly expand the gameplay experience.

Sure, there are still some lingering frustrations involving NPC faction quests and missing characters, but the overall response to the update has been largely positive.

And honestly, if a game lets players ride a tiger into battle while their pet dog attacks enemies beside them, the developers are probably doing something right.
