What Does Refresh Rate Actually Do?

Refresh rate refers to how many times a monitor updates its image every second.

A 240Hz monitor refreshes the screen 240 times per second.

A 320Hz monitor refreshes the screen 320 times per second.

Higher refresh rates improve:

  • Motion smoothness
  • Motion clarity
  • Input responsiveness
  • Tracking during fast movement

In competitive games, smoother motion can help us react faster and track enemies more accurately.

At least in theory.

Can We Actually See the Difference Between 240Hz and 320Hz?

Yes — but the difference is smaller than most marketing suggests.

The jump from:

  • 60Hz to 144Hz = massive improvement
  • 144Hz to 240Hz = noticeable improvement
  • 240Hz to 320Hz = much smaller improvement

This is where the law of diminishing returns becomes very obvious.

Most gamers can immediately feel the difference between 60Hz and 144Hz because motion smoothness improves dramatically.

However, the jump from 240Hz to 320Hz mainly benefits:

  • Professional esports players
  • Highly competitive FPS gamers
  • Players with extremely fast reaction times

For average gamers, the difference exists, but it is subtle.

Very subtle.

The ASUS PG27UCDM uses a QD-OLED panel with an incredibly fast 0.03ms response time.

That response time is dramatically faster than traditional IPS panels.

Because OLED pixels transition almost instantly, ghosting and motion blur are nearly eliminated.

This means a 240Hz OLED monitor can actually look clearer during motion than some higher-refresh IPS monitors.

That sounds strange at first, but response time matters just as much as refresh rate for perceived motion clarity.

However, IPS technology simply cannot fully match OLED’s near-instant pixel response behavior.

So while the Alienware technically refreshes faster at 320Hz, the ASUS OLED often appears cleaner and sharper during real gameplay movement.

Input Lag: Is 320Hz Really Faster?

Technically, yes.

A 320Hz monitor updates frames slightly faster than a 240Hz display, resulting in marginally lower input latency.

Here’s the approximate frame timing difference:

Refresh Rate Frame Time
240Hz 4.16ms per frame
320Hz 3.12ms per frame

That means the difference between 240Hz and 320Hz is roughly 1 millisecond.

For professional esports players competing at the highest level, that tiny difference can matter.

For most gamers?

Our reaction time, internet latency, game engine limitations, and occasional panic button mashing usually matter far more.

240Hz OLED vs 320Hz IPS for Competitive Gaming

If we only play competitive esports titles like:

  • Counter-Strike 2
  • Valorant
  • Overwatch 2
  • Rainbow Six Siege
  • Fortnite

then the Alienware AW2525HM makes a lot of sense.

Its 320Hz refresh rate focuses entirely on competitive utility.

The lower 1080p resolution also helps players maintain extremely high frame rates without requiring an insanely expensive GPU.

This monitor is built for players who care about:

  • Maximum FPS
  • Lowest possible latency
  • Pure competitive focus

The ASUS PG27UCDM, meanwhile, targets “sweaty” gamers who also want beautiful visuals.

It still delivers elite competitive performance while also offering:

  • 4K resolution
  • OLED contrast
  • Outstanding HDR
  • Premium image quality

Basically:

  • Alienware = esports utility machine
  • ASUS OLED = esports luxury edition

Which Monitor Feels Better in Real Games?

In actual gameplay, many gamers end up preferring OLED despite the lower refresh rate.

Why?

Because OLED improves the entire visual experience.

Games look:

  • Sharper
  • More vibrant
  • More immersive
  • Cleaner during motion

The ASUS OLED monitor feels incredibly smooth while also delivering stunning image quality.

Meanwhile, 320Hz IPS monitors mainly prioritize competitive responsiveness above everything else.

That focus works brilliantly for esports, but it feels less impressive during story-driven games, media viewing, or general desktop use.

Is 320Hz Worth It?

For professional or highly competitive gamers, yes.

If we consistently play competitive FPS games at high skill levels, a 320Hz monitor can provide small but meaningful responsiveness advantages.

However, for most gamers, 240Hz already feels incredibly smooth.

At that point, image quality, panel technology, and response time become more important than simply increasing refresh rate further.

This is why many gamers now prefer 240Hz OLED monitors over ultra-high-refresh IPS or TN panels.

OLED simply creates a more complete gaming experience overall.

Which Monitor Should We Buy?

The answer depends entirely on our priorities.

We should choose a 320Hz Fast IPS monitor like the Alienware AW2525HM if:

  • We mainly play competitive esports games
  • We prioritize maximum responsiveness
  • We want extremely high frame rates
  • We care more about utility than visuals

We should choose a 240Hz OLED monitor like the ASUS PG27UCDM if:

  • We want elite gaming visuals
  • We play both competitive and story games
  • We value OLED contrast and HDR
  • We want premium all-around gaming performance

Final Verdict

Yes, the difference between 240Hz and 320Hz is real.

But it is much smaller than the jump from lower refresh rates.

For most gamers, OLED response time and image quality matter more than moving from 240Hz to 320Hz.

The Alienware AW2525HM remains an excellent esports monitor designed for pure competitive speed.

Meanwhile, the ASUS PG27UCDM delivers an incredible balance between elite competitive performance and stunning OLED visuals.

In the end, 320Hz is impressive.

But OLED at 240Hz often feels so good that many gamers stop caring about the extra 80Hz entirely.

Especially once we realize our biggest performance bottleneck was never the monitor.

It was us all along.
